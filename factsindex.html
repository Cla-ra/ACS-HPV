#HPVFact: HPV vaccination is cancer prevention

The human papillomavirus (HPV) causes six types of cancer and is a common infection

9 out of 10 adults – both men and women – are infected with HPV at some point in their lives. Fortunately, we have a vaccine that will prevent this infection from ever occurring and prevent many of the cancers it causes.

Globally, cervical cancer is the 4 th commonest cancer and leading cause of preventable deaths in women. Human papillomavirus (HPV) is the primary cause of cervical cancer.

In 2012, an estimated 530,000 new cases of cervical cancer were reported worldwide. In the same year, 270,000 cervical cancer deaths were reported, with the majority (85%) occurring in developing countries (Globocan, 2012, WHO, 2016).

Sub-Saharan Africa bears one of the highest global burdens of cervical cancer, with an estimated rate of 50 per 100,000 women. In Kenya, cancer is the third leading cause of death and cervical cancer is the leading cause of cancer deaths among women.

Cancer of the cervix is the third most common cancer in India but accounts for the highest number of cancer deaths. The Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR) has projected that there will be 100,000 new diagnoses of cervical cancer in 2016 and about 104,000 by 2020.

This is an unprecedented opportunity to save lives and reduce the HPV cancer burden. HPV vaccines are proven to be safe, effective, and provide lasting protection.
